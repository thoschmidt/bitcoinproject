Bitcoin

Under construction

In his address last month to the DeepSec Security Conference at the Imperial Riding School in Vienna, Austria, Jon Matonis, e-Money researcher and secretary of the Bitcoin Foundation claimed that the fiat currencies of the world were about to face a peer-to-peer network disruptor equivalent to the upheaval that the music and movie industries faced about a decade earlier. The question circulating through many people’s minds has been:

“Is Bitcoin the digital cash that will make this a reality?”

BitTorrent turned the music and movie industries on their heads and profoundly affected digital content distribution through the ability of users to rapidly exchange files over a peer-to-peer internet network. If Bitcoin fits this analogy, then it has the chance to disrupt currency and the world as we know it with incredible destructive and constructive power.

Examining the fundamental ethical and policy implications of bitcoin are crucial towards navigating it to its potential success and directing its power for the good of society.

The Chaotic and Exciting World of Bitcoin

Answering these important ethical and policy questions is challenging. The bitcoin world is rapidly growing and changing. The first bitcoins were only mined in 2009 (“Genesis Block”). Less than four years later, the bitcoin distributed computing network is one of the largest distributed computing networks in the world that eclipses funded projects such as SETI decades its superior (Matonis)

There are currently over $140 million of bitcoins in circulation today (“Market Circulation”)

The rapid rise of bitcoin that makes the bitcoin world precisely so exciting and interesting, also makes it vast, complex, and chaotic. Reuters has called it “the Wild West of finance” as an army of sites of varying credibility have popped up to emulate bitcoin copies financial industry services (O’Leary). In the bitcoin world, venture backed startups like CoinLab (63) run alongside 17-year-old-run exchanges like Bitconica (O’Leary); Bitcoin monetary activists fight central bank tyranny in the same online forums as questionable characters executing ponzi schemes (“Bitcoin ponzi scheme - investors lose $5 million USD in online hedge fund.”); the largest exchange is a converted Magic The Gathering Trading Card hub (Mt. Gox Company Overview); and the latest news and cutting edge opinion rests not only in academic cryptography papers, but on community sites, in online forum posts, and within the blogs of authors with unverified credentials.
